External Threads
– In many assemblies, there is a need for external threads.
While
threading carbide is possible by hard grinding, it reduces the cross sectional strength
due to the notch eﬀect of the threads acting as stress risers.
An alternate approach
is to braze a thin-walled steel or INVAR bushing in place and thread the bushing.
