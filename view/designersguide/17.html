They are resistant down to pH2-3.
In certain solutions, where pH value

Because they have WC as
the hard principle, and nickel and cobalt are similar metals in most respects, their
mechanical and thermal properties are similar to those of straight WC-Co grades.
The pH factor is one of the most important parameters when determining how corrosive
a medium will be.
Other major inﬂuencing factors include temperature and the electric
conductivity of the medium.
The latter is dependent on the ion concentration, i.e.
the
amount of dissolved salts in the solution.
Therefore, it is hard to simply determine how
corrosive a certain medium will be.
No general rules apply to all situations, however, it is
generally accepted that WC-Co cemented carbides should not be exposed to below pH7
or leaching will occur.
For a particular grade, it is recommended that tests be conducted
with the intended medium.
